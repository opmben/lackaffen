# DESIGN_SYSTEM.md - Lack Affen Creative Principles

This document defines the creative direction and design principles for Lack Affen.

It is intentionally principle-led rather than pixel-prescriptive.

---

## 1. Brand Aesthetic

Lack Affen should feel like a professional auto detailing workshop with visible craft, clean results, and local trust.

The visual territory is:
- workshop-premium
- precise
- tactile
- clean
- confident
- local
- image-led
- slightly playful only where the logo earns it

The website should not feel like:
- a cartoon mascot site
- a generic SaaS page
- a dark luxury showroom
- a template-based local business page
- a loud coupon website

Premium here means:
- clean composition
- strong photography
- clear service structure
- polished details
- credible wording
- visible result quality

It does not mean:
- fake exclusivity
- black-and-gold luxury cliches
- inflated claims
- overdesigned effects

---

## 2. Core Visual Idea

The site should feel like a freshly detailed car under controlled workshop light.

Use visual language inspired by:
- paint gloss
- polish movement
- clean microfiber texture
- inspection lighting
- before/after contrast
- precise service checklists
- red workshop stamps
- the brown/red identity from the logo

The monkey mascot and script logo are useful brand memory, but they should not dominate the whole page.

Use the mascot as:
- a logo mark
- small brand detail
- occasional stamped or sign-like motif
- friendly recognition point

Do not use the mascot as:
- giant page decoration
- repeated illustration pattern
- replacement for real vehicle photography
- reason to make the site childish

---

## 3. Color Direction

Base palette should come from the brand and workshop context:
- warm off-white / clean shop surface
- deep espresso brown from the logo
- lacquer red from the stamp
- charcoal / tire black for contrast
- restrained metal or graphite neutrals

Use red as an accent, not a flood color.

Use brown as brand grounding, not as a muddy dominant theme.

Avoid:
- purple or blue AI gradients
- black/gold luxury cliches
- neon detailing aesthetics
- one-note brown/orange pages
- pure black or pure white where a tinted neutral works better

Use modern color functions where practical, preferably OKLCH for custom CSS tokens.

---

## 4. Typography

Typography should feel practical, confident, and crafted.

Use:
- a distinctive display face for high-impact headings
- a readable body face for service details and form content
- strong hierarchy and tight copy

Avoid:
- generic system-only typography as the final design
- overused SaaS fonts as the only visual identity
- long uppercase paragraphs
- tiny gray text
- overly elegant editorial type that fights the workshop context

Type should support fast scanning:
- service names
- price levels
- vehicle categories
- appointment inquiry steps
- contact details

---

## 5. Imagery

Real photography is central to the site.

Priority images:
- exterior paint gloss
- polishing in progress
- interior cleaning details
- before/after interior results
- ceramic coating or water beading
- workshop environment
- team-at-work details
- close-ups of wheels, leather, glass, trim, and paint

Photography should be:
- bright enough to inspect results
- crisp
- specific
- local and real
- not stock-like

Avoid:
- dark, blurred, atmospheric images where users cannot inspect the result
- generic car stock photos
- AI-generated cars as proof of work
- decorative images that do not show service quality

---

## 6. Layout And Composition

The layout should be direct and conversion-oriented.

Prioritize:
- a strong hero with immediate service clarity
- visible appointment path
- service groups that are easy to compare
- proof through photos and reviews
- local contact and route utility
- mobile-first speed

Use asymmetry and visual rhythm where it improves quality, but keep the service information easy to understand.

Avoid:
- endless equal card grids
- generic icon + headline + paragraph repetition
- nested cards
- long text-first sections
- decorative dividers that do not help scanning

---

## 7. CTA Rules

The only visible CTA text is:

`Termin anfragen`

Allowed uses:
- hero primary action
- sticky mobile action
- service section action
- contact section submit-oriented action

Do not use:
- `Gutschein kaufen`
- `Jetzt kaufen`
- `Mehr erfahren` as a prominent CTA
- competing button labels that dilute the inquiry path

Gift cards can be reachable through low-emphasis navigation or footer links, but they must not visually compete with appointment inquiry.

---

## 8. Motion

Motion should feel like controlled workshop precision.

Good uses:
- subtle page load reveals
- before/after interactions
- service group transitions
- form feedback
- hover states with tactile response

Avoid:
- gimmick mascot animation
- excessive scroll theatrics
- motion that hides information
- heavy effects on mobile

Respect reduced-motion preferences.

---

## 9. Explicit No-Gos

Do not use:
- fake testimonials
- fake review totals
- fake opening hours
- fake guarantees
- generic SaaS gradients
- gradient text
- oversized mascot decoration
- coupon-site styling
- all-dark luxury showroom styling
- glassmorphism copied from Metz & Partner
- feature grids that could belong to any business

The design must feel designed for auto detailing in Bad Salzig, not adapted from an agency template.
